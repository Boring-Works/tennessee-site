## Letter from Governor Blount to Secretary Knox

_November 3, 1790_

Sir,

<passage id="territory-organization">I have the honor to inform you that I have taken measures for organizing the government of this territory. The inhabitants appear well disposed to support the authority of the United States, and I flatter myself that tranquility will soon be established on a permanent basis.</passage>

The militia of the settlements is in tolerable order, though much wants to be done to bring them to a proper state of discipline. <passage id="militia-state">The men are generally good marksmen and accustomed to the woods, but they require officers capable of directing their movements in a systematic manner.</passage>

I have received information that the Cherokee towns are disposed to peace, and I shall lose no time in opening a negotiation with them. <passage id="cherokee-negotiations">It is my opinion that a general treaty, conducted with proper attention to the interests of both parties, may establish lasting harmony between the citizens of this territory and the Cherokee Nation.</passage>

I have the honor to be, with the greatest respect, your most obedient servant,

William Blount
