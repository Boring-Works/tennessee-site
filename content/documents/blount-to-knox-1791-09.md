## Letter from Governor Blount to Secretary Knox

_September 12, 1791_

Sir,

<passage id="peace-maintained">Since the conclusion of the treaty, peace has been generally maintained on the frontier. The Cherokee chiefs have exerted themselves to restrain their young men, and I have taken measures to prevent encroachments by the settlers.</passage>

I am making preparations to remove my residence to a more central location. <passage id="knoxville-plans">A site has been selected on the Holston, which I propose to call Knoxville in honor of the Secretary of War. It will afford better facilities for the transaction of public business and for communication with the Cherokee country.</passage>

The treaty goods have been distributed to the satisfaction of the Indians. <passage id="goods-distribution">They appear pleased with the articles received and have expressed their determination to live in friendship with the United States. I am hopeful that the peace thus established may be permanent.</passage>

I have the honor to be, Sir, your obedient servant,

William Blount
