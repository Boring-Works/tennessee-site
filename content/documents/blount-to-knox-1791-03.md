## Letter from Governor Blount to Secretary Knox

_March 20, 1791_

Sir,

<passage id="treaty-location">I have fixed upon White's Fort, on the Holston River, as the place for holding the proposed treaty with the Cherokee Nation. It is centrally located and accessible to the principal chiefs from both the Overhill and Chickamauga towns.</passage>

I have dispatched messengers to invite the chiefs to meet me in early summer. <passage id="chiefs-invited">Both the Hanging Maw, principal chief of the Overhill towns, and the Bloody Fellow, a leader of great influence among the Chickamauga, have signified their intention to attend. Their presence is essential to any lasting agreement.</passage>

The presents intended for distribution at the treaty are being assembled. <passage id="treaty-presents">These goods, consisting of blankets, cloth, implements of husbandry, and other articles useful to the Indians, are necessary to demonstrate the good faith of the United States and to compensate the Cherokee for the lands they may cede.</passage>

I have the honor to be, Sir, your obedient servant,

William Blount
