## The Knoxville Gazette

### Volume 1, Number 10 - January 7, 1792

---

### The New Year

<passage id="new-year-reflection">As we enter upon a new year, we have abundant reason for gratitude. The peace concluded with the Cherokee Nation has opened to settlement a vast extent of fertile country. The federal government affords us protection, and our territory advances in population and improvements with a rapidity unexampled in the history of new settlements.</passage>

### From the Capital

<passage id="federal-news">Letters from Philadelphia inform us that Congress has under consideration various measures for the benefit of the western territories. It is expected that provision will be made for roads and other internal improvements which will greatly facilitate our commerce with the old states.</passage>

### Advertisement

<passage id="ferry-notice">Ferry across the Holston. The subscriber has established a ferry at the mouth of First Creek, where travelers and their effects will be conveyed across the river at the following rates: Man and horse, one shilling; Footman, six pence; Wagon and team, three shillings.</passage>
