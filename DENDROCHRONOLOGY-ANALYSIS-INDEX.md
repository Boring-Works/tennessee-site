## DENDROCHRONOLOGY IN CONTEXT

### Why This Matters for Rocky Mount

The site's core claim is: **"Tennessee's first territorial capital"** (1790-1791)

This is documented and defensible.

The Jackson tradition is: **"Jackson visited in 1788"**

This is consistent but unverified and dendrochronologically constrained.

These are SEPARATE claims. The capital claim is solid. The Jackson claim is uncertain.

### The Honest Interpretation

A site can say:

> "Andrew Jackson's tradition says he visited in 1788. Multiple historical sources repeat this story. However, the buildings you see were built in 1826-1830. We don't have primary documentation of his visit, but the tradition is worth knowing about."

This is true, honest, and maintains trust.

### The Problematic Interpretation (Now Corrected)

A site should NOT say:

> "Verified: Andrew Jackson stayed here in 1788"

This was the problem before. Now it's fixed.
