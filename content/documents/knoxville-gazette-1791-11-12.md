## The Knoxville Gazette

### Volume 1, Number 2 - November 12, 1791

---

### By the President of the United States

<passage id="proclamation-published">We are authorized to publish the following proclamation, received by express from the seat of federal government: The Treaty of Peace and Friendship, concluded at this place on the second of July last between Governor Blount and the Cherokee Nation, has been ratified by the President with the advice and consent of the Senate.</passage>

This happy event ensures the peace of our frontier and opens to settlement the fertile lands lying between the present establishments and the Cherokee boundary.

### Local Intelligence

<passage id="knoxville-growth">The town of Knoxville continues to flourish. Several new buildings are in progress, including a courthouse and jail. The Governor's house, situated on a commanding eminence, overlooks the river and affords a handsome prospect of the surrounding country.</passage>

### Prices Current

Corn, per bushel: 2s 6d
Wheat, per bushel: 5s
Bacon, per pound: 6d
Whiskey, per gallon: 3s
