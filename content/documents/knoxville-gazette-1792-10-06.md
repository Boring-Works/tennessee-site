## The Knoxville Gazette

### Volume 1, Number 49 - October 6, 1792

---

### Harvest Report

<passage id="abundant-harvest">The harvest of this year has been uncommonly abundant. The crops of corn, wheat, and tobacco exceed anything before known in this territory. The industry of the inhabitants, combined with the fertility of the soil and the blessings of Providence, has produced a plenty which ensures comfort through the approaching winter.</passage>

### Commerce

<passage id="trade-routes">The navigation of the Tennessee and Holston rivers affords a valuable outlet for the produce of this territory. Boats loaded with flour, whiskey, and other articles descend regularly to the settlements on the Cumberland and thence to the markets of New Orleans.</passage>

### Notice

<passage id="tax-collection">Notice is hereby given that the collector of taxes for Knox County will attend at the courthouse in Knoxville on the first Monday of November next to receive the taxes due for the present year. Prompt payment is requested.</passage>
