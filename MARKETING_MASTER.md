## TOUR GUIDE SCRIPTS

**SOURCE:** `docs/marketing-system/GUIDE-SCRIPTS.md`

### The 5 Moment Model

Most tours follow this path. These are the natural moments to mention upcoming events:

1. **Weaving Cabin** → Woolly Days (April)
2. **Blacksmith Forge** → Blacksmith Workshops (monthly)
3. **Main House (Blount Room)** → Lectures, July 4 ceremony
4. **Kitchen** → Harvest Fest (October), Hearth Cooking
5. **Gift Shop** → ALL events, next visit planning

### Moment #1: Weaving Cabin Script (Short Version)

> "Yeah, and she did it constantly. Not just because she wanted to—because her family would freeze otherwise. No stores, no buying blankets. Barsheba made everything.
>
> Honestly, it's pretty eye-opening once you try it. We actually do an event called Woolly Days where visitors get their hands in the work. Spinning wheel, carding, the whole thing. Most people come expecting to watch. They end up making something they take home. If you're here in spring, it's worth it."

### Common Objections & Rebuttals

**"That sounds exhausting."**
> "It is. That's the point. Once you do it for two hours, you have a new respect for women who did it for life. Plus, it's actually kind of meditative. The rhythm is calming."

**"I don't have time for a two-day event."**
> "Totally fair. This tour gives you the overview. Woolly Days is for people who want to go deeper. Most folks don't need both—the tour is complete on its own. But if you find yourself thinking about Barsheba later, you know where to find us."

### Gift Shop Follow-Up

**When this guest reaches checkout:**

> "While I'm ringing you up, did [guide name] mention Woolly Days? We do that in late April. Most people who come on a tour like this end up coming back for it."

## QUICK REFERENCE: CORE SYSTEMS

### 1. Brand Voice

**BE:** Credible. Confident. Clear. Historic but not dusty.
**AVOID:** Desperation. Hyperbole. Pushiness.

### 2. Primary Hooks (Use These)

- "Stand where they stood." (Universal CTA)
- "Before there was a Tennessee, there was this ground." (Welcome Hook)
- "Tennessee starts here." (Brand Tagline)
- "Where Tennessee's government began." (Permanent Identity)

### 3. Tour-to-Event Conversion

**Process:**
1. Guide mentions ONE event during tour (natural moment)
2. Gift shop reinforces with printed cards
3. Guest takes card home
4. Email follow-up (if captured)
5. Booking conversion within 3 months

**Target:** 25-30% of tour-goers book an event within 3 months.

**Ground First. House Second.**

Visual hierarchy must match brand hierarchy. The ground is the artifact.
