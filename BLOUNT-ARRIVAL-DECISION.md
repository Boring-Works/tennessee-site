## Primary Source Evidence

**Document:** William Blount to Henry Knox (Secretary of War)
**Written:** October 20, 1790
**Archive:** State Archives of North Carolina, John Gray Blount Papers, PC.193
**Published:** Keith, Alice Barnwell, ed. _The John Gray Blount Papers, Vol. II (1790-1795)_. Raleigh: State Department of Archives and History, 1959. pp. 127-128

**Exact Quote:**

> "On the 11th instant, I arrived in this country, and was received with every mark of attention and gladness that I could have wished."

**Date Referenced:** "The 11th instant" = October 11, 1790

This is **Blount's own letter** written 9 days after his arrival. He explicitly states he arrived on "the 11th instant" (contemporary date notation meaning "the 11th of this month").

## Historical Notes

### The Context

- **Southwest Territory created:** May 26, 1790
- **Blount nominated as Governor:** June 7, 1790
- **Blount confirmed by Senate:** June 8, 1790
- **Blount arrives at Rocky Mount:** **October 11, 1790** ← PRIMARY SOURCE DATE
- **Blount writes "Glass Windows" letter:** October 20, 1790

### Why This Matters

Blount's arrival at Rocky Mount established it as the **first capital of the Southwest Territory** - the governmental entity that would become Tennessee. This is one of the most significant dates in Tennessee history.

**Primary source wins.** When the governor himself writes "On the 11th instant, I arrived," that date is definitive.
