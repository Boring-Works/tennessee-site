## The Knoxville Gazette

### Volume 1, Number 24 - April 14, 1792

---

### Agriculture

<passage id="spring-planting">The season for planting is now arrived, and the industrious inhabitants of this territory are busily employed in cultivating their lands. The soil in most parts is admirably adapted to the production of corn, wheat, tobacco, and cotton, and the crops promise to be abundant.</passage>

### Advertisement

<passage id="store-goods">Just received from Philadelphia and Baltimore, and for sale at the subscriber's store in Knoxville: Dry goods of various kinds, hardware, cutlery, queensware, medicines, books and stationery, and a variety of other articles suited to the wants of the inhabitants of this territory.</passage>

### Indian Affairs

<passage id="continued-peace">The peace with the Cherokee Nation continues. Several of the chiefs have visited Knoxville this spring to receive their proportion of the goods stipulated in the treaty. They express satisfaction with the conduct of the government and promise to continue in friendship.</passage>
